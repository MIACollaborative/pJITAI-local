Model Settings / Proximal Outcome Attributes

  • General Explaination
  • HeartSteps Example

We use the attributes of the proximal outcome to check if the API receives inappropriate values from your mobile app. Please read [tutorial] for more details.

Please notice that if your proximal outcome needs to be transformed, the transformation needs to be implemented in the mobile application; the algorithm will not do the transformation.

In HeartSteps, the proximal outcome of interest is the log-transformed 30-minute step count after each decision point. To avoid minus infinity (recall that loge(0) becomes minus infinity), we added 0.5 to the step count before the transformation, i.e., loge(next 30-minute step count+0.5). Our assumption was that people walk between 0 and 3000 steps in the next 30 minutes. Hence, the minimum value of the (log) 30-minute step count is loge(0+0.5)=-0.69. The maximum value of the (log) 30-minute step count is loge(3000+0.5)=8.0. These values are continuous.
