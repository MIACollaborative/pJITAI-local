As data accrues, the probability of sending Intervention Option A (when Intervention Option A is eligible) will change but will never go below the lower bound or above the upper bound.

To ensure a continual update of personalization, you cannot choose the intervention probability lower/upper bound during personalization to be 0 or 1. If you are concerned about burden or habituation, we suggest that you choose a smaller upper bound.

Here, we choose the intervention probability during the personalization period to be between 0.1 and 0.8. Notice that with 5 decision points/day, an intervention probability of 0.8 corresponds to an average of 5 x 0.8 = 4 activity suggestion messages per day. If you want to avoid sending four activity suggestions a day, you can consider 1) lowering the upper bound or 2) adding “receiving more than three activity suggestions in the same day” as an ineligibility criterion.
